history of Cocktails

From as long as human history existed,humans have been drinking alcohol, and from since ,the've been mixing spirits with honey, seawater and spices. In the early 1860s the word cocktail has been introduced, by a bartender named Jerry Thomas who published his book The Bar Manual in 1862. Traditionally cocktails are made with the mixture of spirits, juices, sugar and bitters Scroll down to learn how to use the tools to make Classic Cocktails, like a professional bartender

Tools

To make classic drinks bartenders require more than just spirits, mixers and the right ingredients.They need the right tools to make drinks consistent ,quicky and efficiently.

  • Bottle Opener

    The most important tool for any bartender,bottle openers
    are cruicial as every beer bottle you will see will be capped.

  • Cocktail Shaker

    Ingredients are placed in the sealed stainless steel shaker, typically
    liquor, syrups, fruit juices, and ice.

  • Cocktail Strainers

    You'll need one of these to create cold ice-free drinks.
    The strainer is placed over the mouth of the shaker,with the small holes
    letting the drink pass through, leaving the ice in the shaker.

  • Jigger

    Acts as a cocktail measuring cup. Jiggers are dual-sided and help
    bartenders cut down on overpouring and remain consistent .

  • Peelers

    For those who like garnishes,peelers
    are important as you can use the essential oils from orange lemon
    or limes skins for your drinks.

  • Bar Spoons

    Bar spoons gently stir the ingredients to just
    the right dilution without breaking down the ice.
